Before you begin

Informed Consent

Study: Compositional Intent Inference in Advertisements: Evaluating Multimodal LLMs with Structured Decomposition (IRB Reference: NCR267426)

Principal Investigator: Rebecca Hwa, PhD (rebecca.hwa@gwu.edu)
Study Team: Shiza Ali (shiza.ali@gwu.edu), Alice Dragnea (alice.dragnea@gwu.edu)
GW Office of Human Research: ohrirb@gwu.edu

Purpose

This study looks at how people interpret the intended message of advertisement-like images, including images where brand names, logos, slogans, or other identifying text have been removed or obscured. Your responses may be compared with responses generated by multimodal AI systems asked to interpret the same images.

What you will do

You will view a series of images one at a time. For each image, you'll answer two brief questions about what you think the ad is trying to communicate and why. There are no right or wrong answers — we're interested in your own interpretation. Some images have had brand names, logos, slogans, or other identifying text removed or obscured as part of the study design; please answer based only on what appears in the image shown to you.

Time commitment

Each image takes about a minute or two. You may annotate as few or as many images as you like — there is no minimum or maximum.

Voluntary participation

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may stop at any point without penalty simply by closing this page.

Risks

Risks are minimal. You may experience mild boredom, fatigue, or uncertainty while interpreting images. Some images may be ambiguous or contain ordinary advertisement-like content. Images have been screened to avoid highly sensitive, graphic, explicit, hateful, or disturbing content.

Benefits & compensation

You are not expected to receive a direct personal benefit, and no compensation is provided for this round of annotation. Your responses will help evaluate how well AI systems interpret persuasive media compared to humans.

Confidentiality

This survey does not collect your name, email, or other direct identifiers. An anonymous ID is generated in your browser only to prevent duplicate annotations of the same image. Responses are stored securely and reported only in aggregate or de-identified form in any publication or presentation.

Questions

Contact the study team above with any questions about the study, or GW's Office of Human Research for questions about your rights as a participant.
