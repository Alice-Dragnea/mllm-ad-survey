Before you begin

Informed Consent

Study: Rhetorical Analysis of Persuasive Media and Modality Bias in MLLMs

Principal Investigator: Rebecca Hwa, PhD (rebecca.hwa@gwu.edu)
Study Team: Shiza Ali (shiza.ali@gwu.edu), Alice Dragnea (alice.dragnea@gwu.edu)
GW Office of Human Research: ohrirb@gwu.edu

Purpose

The purpose of this study is to collect human interpretations of advertisements which have been modified from their original versions. Your responses will be compared with those generated by Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), for the same image. This will help researchers investigate the types of components MLLMs rely on when interpreting non-literal media, and how their responses change when certain cues are removed, compared to humans. In the future the data might also be used for AI model training and validation purposes.

What you will do

If you agree to participate in this survey, you will view a series of images one at a time. For each image, you will be asked to answer two brief questions about what you think the ad is trying to communicate, in your own words, and without the help of generative AI tools. Some images have had brand names, logos, slogans, or other identifying text removed or obscured as part of the study design; you will respond using only what appears in the image shown to you.

Time commitment

You may respond for as many images as you like, at your own pace. We estimate a typical response may take about one minute.

Risks & Confidentiality

Risks are minimal. You may experience a range of emotions or reactions while interpreting images, from surprise, to uncertainty, fatigue, or boredom.

This survey does not collect your name, email, or other direct identifiers. An anonymous ID and timestamp is generated in your browser only to prevent duplicate annotations of the same image.

Benefits & Compensation

You will not be compensated for your participation, and are not expected to receive a direct personal benefit from this. Your responses, however, will help researchers better understand how AI systems interpret persuasive media compared to humans.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may choose not to participate by closing this page. If you have questions about the study, you please contact the principal investigator or research team.
